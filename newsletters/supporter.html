Familial testing and diabetes

ADC is investigating 'Familial testing and diabetes' - and we want to know what YOU think.

If you have a family member with diabetes, have you considered getting the rest of your family tested to see if they also have the condition?

If your family hasn't been touched by diabetes, is diabetes screening something you would think worthwhile? Is it a cost factor?

Whatever the case, whatever your story or your opinion, we'd love to know what you think. We will be presenting the results of this survey at our Diabetes and Sustainability Forum in July.

This is your chance to be heard! Email web@australiandiabetescouncil.com

Grapefruit and interaction with medications

Grapefruit is a nutritious fruit, which can be a part of a healthy diet. However, grapefruit and grapefruit juice have the potential to interact with a number of medicines. A review article published in the Canadian Medical Association Journal (November 2012) reveals more than 85 medicines that can interact with grapefruit, with 43 of these having potentially serious consequences.

Ongoing survey about the use of medications in type 2 diabetes

A study by the Faculty of Pharmacy, University of Sydney, to determine how people with type 2 diabetes use their medication and  the problems they experience is still ongoing.  To date there has been a good response and we thank those members who have already responded.  However, for the study to be successful we need more members to complete the survey. 
 
The study is supported by Australian Diabetes Council and we encourage you to take part. The information will help us deliver better services to our clients with type 2 diabetes.
